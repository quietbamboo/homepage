Introduction

I'm currently a 5th year Ph.D. student in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor.
I work with Professor Z. Morley Mao.
My research interests include smartphone related research topics, such as the performance and power related research for 3G/4G networks.
I'm also interested in designing and implementing systems that give smartphone users better experiences.

My favourite computer game for my undergraduate age is DotA, though I haven't played it for quite a while.
I like traveling, photography, ice skating, and am learning piano at my spare time.
My favourite sport is table tennis and I'm honored to be a member of the University of Michigan Table Tennis Team.
I'm also interested in developing cool iPhone/Android apps.

Projects

I'm the lead developer of MobiPerf, which evolves from the old 3GTest. It is run by hundreds and thousands of smartphone users to understanding performance and networking policy on commercial 3G/4G netorks. The project is open-sourced here. We are currently working on the next release of MobiPerf and the github repo is here.

In 2011, MobiPerf has won People's Choice Award and Open Internet App Reward in the FCC Open Internet Apps Challenge.
